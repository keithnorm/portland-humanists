Dr. Sarah Johnson is a clinical psychologist with over 20 years of experience studying belief formation and cognitive biases. Her recent book, "The Believing Brain," explores how our minds construct and maintain beliefs, even in the face of contradictory evidence.

In this presentation, she will discuss:

- The neuroscience of belief formation
- How childhood experiences shape our worldview
- The role of social influence and group dynamics
- Cognitive biases and their impact on reasoning
- Strategies for critical thinking and belief examination

This promises to be a fascinating exploration of one of the most fundamental aspects of human psychology. Whether you're interested in understanding your own beliefs better or learning how to engage more effectively with others, this presentation will provide valuable insights.
